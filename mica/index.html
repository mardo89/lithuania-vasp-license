What MiCA changed (and why it matters in 2026)

Before MiCA, EU crypto regulation was a patchwork of national VASP frameworks (Lithuania, Estonia, France PSAN, Germany BaFin Crypto Custody, Malta VFA, etc.) with no automatic cross-border recognition. A Lithuanian VASP could not legally serve EU customers in Germany without separate German registration.

MiCA harmonized this. Now a single CASP authorization in any EU member state grants the right to provide crypto services across all 27 EU + EEA countries via passporting. The trade-off: MiCA imposes uniform minimum standards (capital, governance, conduct rules, market abuse, white papers for issuers) that are stricter than some pre-MiCA national frameworks.

For Lithuania specifically:

  • Pre-MiCA Lithuanian VASP framework — closed to new applicants since 30 December 2024. Existing VASPs grandfathered until 30 June 2026.
  • MiCA CASP framework — only path for new authorizations since 30 December 2024. Bank of Lithuania reviews under MiCA Article 62.
  • Capital requirements — increased from light Lithuanian VASP standards to MiCA's €50K–€150K paid-up + maintained.
  • Substance expectations — Bank of Lithuania expects local Lithuanian MLRO + at least one Lithuanian-resident board member.
  • Market abuse + transparency — Class 3 (trading platforms) trigger pre/post-trade transparency obligations.

The MiCA timeline you need to know

  • 30 June 2023: MiCA published in Official Journal of the EU.
  • 30 June 2024: Title III (Asset-Referenced Tokens) and Title IV (E-Money Tokens) entered force.
  • 30 December 2024: Full MiCA enforcement begins. CASP authorization required for new entrants.
  • 30 December 2024 – 30 June 2026: 18-month grandfathering period for pre-MiCA EU VASPs.
  • 30 June 2026 (CRITICAL): Grandfathering ends. All EU crypto businesses must hold CASP authorization or cease operations.

Lithuania CASP application — what Bank of Lithuania reviews

Under MiCA Article 62, Bank of Lithuania reviews CASP applications across these dimensions:

  1. Business plan + financial projections — 3-year forward-looking model with credible commercial assumptions
  2. Capital adequacy — paid-up capital deposited; ongoing capital ratios maintained
  3. AML/CFT framework — 5AMLD/6AMLD compliant; risk assessment specific to applicant; FCIS reporting workflow
  4. Governance arrangements — board composition, conflicts of interest policy, internal audit, MLRO independence
  5. Key personnel fit-and-proper — directors, MLRO, IT/security officer, ≥10% shareholders all reviewed individually
  6. IT and operational risk management — cybersecurity, custody segregation, business continuity, outsourcing arrangements
  7. Customer protection — terms of service, complaint handling, marketing communications, white papers (where applicable)
  8. Market abuse prevention (Class 3) — surveillance, transparency, conflicts management for trading platforms

Total review timeline: 20–25 weeks under MiCA Article 62 (3-month completeness check + extended substantive review). Faster for Class 1 simple applications; longer for Class 3 trading platforms.

EU passporting — how it works under MiCA

Once Lithuania CASP-authorized, you can provide crypto services across:

  • 27 EU member states (Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden)
  • 3 EEA countries (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway)

Passport activation per country:

  1. File passport notification with Bank of Lithuania (specify receiving country + which services)
  2. Bank of Lithuania forwards to receiving competent authority within 1 month
  3. 30-day waiting period after receipt by receiving authority
  4. You can provide services in receiving country

Each country requires a separate notification, but the underlying authorization is one Lithuanian CASP. Most operators passport into 5–10 priority countries (Germany, France, Spain, Netherlands, Italy, Poland) at launch and add more as customer demand develops.

VASP-to-CASP transition for existing Lithuanian VASPs

If you hold an existing Lithuanian VASP registered before 30 December 2024, you have until 30 June 2026 to transition to MiCA CASP. Transition steps:

  1. Capital adjustment — top up to €50K (Class 1), €125K (Class 2), or €150K (Class 3) if not already at MiCA threshold.
  2. AML program update — adapt your existing AML program to MiCA Article 39 requirements (largely overlap with 5AMLD/6AMLD).
  3. Key personnel re-fit-and-proper — Bank of Lithuania re-reviews directors and MLRO under MiCA standards.
  4. IT and governance updates — implement MiCA-specific provisions (custody segregation, conflicts of interest, market abuse for Class 3).
  5. CASP application submission — formal application via Bank of Lithuania portal. Faster than greenfield (~3-4 month review for transitions).
  6. Authorization granted — VASP terminated, CASP active. EU passporting becomes available.

FAQ — Lithuania MiCA license

What is MiCA and how does it apply in Lithuania?

MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation, EU Regulation 2023/1114) is the EU's harmonized crypto framework that took effect 30 December 2024. It creates three CASP service classes with capital requirements of €50K (Class 1), €125K (Class 2), or €150K (Class 3). Lithuania implements MiCA via Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos Bankas) as the competent authority. CASPs authorized in Lithuania can passport their services across all 27 EU member states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway (EEA).

When does MiCA grandfathering end in Lithuania?

30 June 2026. Lithuanian VASPs registered before 30 December 2024 (when MiCA enforcement began) have an 18-month grandfathering period to obtain CASP authorization. After 30 June 2026, all Lithuanian crypto businesses must hold CASP authorization or cease operations.

What are the 3 MiCA CASP service classes?

Class 1 (€50,000 capital): reception/transmission of orders, advice on crypto-assets, portfolio management. Class 2 (€125,000 capital): Class 1 + custody and administration, exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto, execution of orders, placing of crypto-assets. Class 3 (€150,000 capital): Class 2 + operation of trading platform (multilateral order book). Most crypto exchanges target Class 2; trading platforms target Class 3.

Does Lithuania CASP grant EU passport automatically?

Almost. CASP authorization grants the right to passport, but you must file separate notifications per receiving EU member state. Each notification takes ~30 days from receiving competent authority's confirmation before you can provide services in that country. Most CASPs passport into 5–10 priority countries at launch and add more as demand grows.

Why is Lithuania the most popular MiCA jurisdiction?

Three reasons: (1) cheapest setup in EU at $50K–$120K vs Cyprus $80K–$200K, Germany $200K+, France $250K+; (2) fastest reviews — Bank of Lithuania has more crypto experience and clearer published guidance; (3) deepest crypto banking ecosystem — Lithuanian EMIs (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome) onboard CASPs in 1–2 weeks vs other EU jurisdictions taking months.
