The 4-stage authorization process

  1. Lithuanian UAB + capital (weeks 1–6): Form UAB, open Lithuanian bank account, deposit €125K–€150K, auditor confirmation.
  2. Application package (weeks 4–14): Build 200–500 page application — business plan, AML program, IT framework, key personnel fit-and-proper.
  3. Bank of Lithuania submission + review (weeks 14–40): 3-month completeness check + extended substantive review under MiCA Article 62.
  4. Authorization + EU passport (weeks 40–42): CASP authorization granted. Notify Bank of Lithuania of EU member states for passporting.

EU passport: serve 27 countries from Lithuania

Once Lithuania CASP-authorized, you can passport into other EU member states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway. Process per receiving country:

  1. File passport notification with Bank of Lithuania (specify receiving country + services)
  2. Bank of Lithuania forwards to receiving competent authority within 1 month
  3. 30-day waiting period after receipt
  4. You can provide services in receiving country

Each member state requires separate notification, but the underlying authorization is one Lithuanian CASP. Most exchanges passport into 5–10 priority countries (Germany, France, Spain, Netherlands, Italy, Poland) at launch.

Key personnel — fit-and-proper requirements

Bank of Lithuania scrutinizes key personnel (board, MLRO, IT officer, ≥10% shareholders). For an exchange specifically:

  • Board members: 5+ years financial-services or crypto experience expected
  • MLRO: 3+ years AML experience, recognized credential (CAMS, ICA Diploma)
  • IT/cybersecurity officer: often combined with MLRO for smaller exchanges; separate for larger
  • Ownership chain: all UBOs ≥10% must clear fit-and-proper
  • Local Lithuanian presence: at least 1 board member or MLRO based in Lithuania

FAQ — Lithuania crypto exchange license

What CASP class does a Lithuania crypto exchange need?

Class 2 (€125,000 capital) for standard spot crypto exchange (custody + exchange + execution). Class 3 (€150,000 capital) if you operate a multilateral trading platform / order book exchange. Most international crypto exchanges target Class 2 unless they specifically run an order-book MTF. Class 1 (€50,000) only covers advisory/portfolio services and is rarely the right choice for an exchange.

How much does a Lithuania crypto exchange license cost?

Total setup runs $50,000–$120,000 in year one (excluding capital). Bank of Lithuania fee €5K–€10K, AML/KYC program €15K–€30K, legal counsel €10K–€25K, Lithuanian UAB formation €5K–€10K, Lithuanian MLRO + substance €15K–€50K. Plus €125,000 paid-up capital (Class 2) or €150,000 (Class 3). Annual maintenance $20,000–$50,000.

How long does Lithuania crypto exchange authorization take?

4–8 months end-to-end. Pre-submission stages take 2–3 months. Bank of Lithuania review under MiCA Article 62 takes 20–25 weeks (3-month completeness check + extended substantive review). Class 3 trading platforms take 7–9 months due to additional market-abuse surveillance documentation.

Does Lithuania CASP grant EU exchange passport?

Yes. Lithuania CASP authorization grants the right to provide crypto-exchange services across 27 EU member states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway (EEA). Activated via notification to Bank of Lithuania per receiving country. Each notification takes ~30 days from receiving authority's confirmation.

What's the substance requirement for a Lithuania crypto exchange?

Bank of Lithuania expects meaningful local Lithuanian substance: at least 1 board member or senior officer with Lithuanian residence, plus a Lithuanian-based MLRO. Real Lithuanian office (not virtual). Bank of Lithuania conducts site visits during examinations. Plan for $15K–$50K/year in substance costs.
