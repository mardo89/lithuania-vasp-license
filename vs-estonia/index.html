Lithuania vs Estonia Crypto License 2026: MiCA CASP Comparison

Two Baltic neighbors, both EU members, both MiCA-compliant, both granting the same EU passport. Yet Lithuania has become the default Baltic CASP jurisdiction in 2025–2026 — and existing Estonian VASPs are migrating in record numbers. Here's why.

Why operators migrated from Estonia to Lithuania (2022–2026)

Estonia was the original EU crypto-licensing hub since 2017. By 2020 it had ~1,500+ licensed VASPs. Then a series of high-profile failures (Estonia-licensed exchanges with weak governance) prompted RAB to tighten significantly:

  • 2022: Higher own-funds requirements (€125K–€350K depending on activity)
  • 2022: Mandatory local Estonian-resident director
  • 2023: Mandatory physical Estonian office (not virtual)
  • 2023: RAB approval of all directors and UBOs (fit-and-proper)
  • 2024: Tighter banking — Estonian banks raised KYC bars on crypto

Result: hundreds of Estonian VASPs revoked or wound down. Many operators that couldn't justify Estonian substance migrated to Lithuania, where Bank of Lithuania applied lighter-touch substance scrutiny and the EMI ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome) provided easier banking.

When Lithuania wins

  1. You don't have a real Estonian presence. Lithuania's substance bar is significantly lower. If you can't commit to a Tallinn office + local Estonian director, Lithuania is the only realistic Baltic CASP option.
  2. Banking ease matters. Lithuanian EMIs (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome) onboard crypto CASPs in 1–2 weeks. Estonian banking has tightened — expect 4–8 weeks and budget for 1–2 rejections.
  3. You're a new applicant (post-Dec 2024). Bank of Lithuania has cleaner published guidance for first-time CASP applicants. Estonia's RAB is more substance-skeptical of newcomers.
  4. You want predictable timelines. Lithuania reviews are highly procedural under MiCA Article 62. Estonia is similarly procedural but applies stricter substance review at the back end.
  5. You're transitioning a non-Estonian VASP into MiCA. Lithuania CASP transition is significantly smoother than starting fresh with Estonia.

When Estonia wins

  1. You're already Estonian-domiciled (or willing to be). If your founder, team, or operations are genuinely in Estonia, the substance bar is met automatically and Estonia's tax structure becomes attractive.
  2. Retained-earnings tax matters. Estonia's 0% on retained earnings is unique in the EU. For crypto businesses reinvesting profits, capital can compound without annual tax drag (Lithuania charges 15%).
  3. You're transitioning an existing Estonian VASP. If you already have Estonian VASP, transitioning to CASP within Estonia avoids the wind-down + new-jurisdiction setup cost. Must be done before 30 June 2026 grandfathering ends.
  4. Pioneer-jurisdiction credibility matters. Estonia was the world's first crypto-licensing jurisdiction (2017). Some institutional clients value the Estonian VASP/CASP brand for that historical reason.
  5. You want a one-jurisdiction Baltic operation. Both work — but Estonia consolidates corporate, tax, and operational base in one country if you can commit.

Migration path: Estonia VASP → Lithuania CASP

If you hold an existing Estonian VASP and the substance requirements are unworkable, the standard migration path is:

  1. Form Lithuanian UAB in parallel (1–2 weeks).
  2. Deposit €125K capital in Lithuanian bank.
  3. Submit Lithuania CASP application using your existing AML program (adapted to Bank of Lithuania format) — saves 4–6 weeks vs greenfield.
  4. Operate both entities in parallel during the 4–8 month Lithuania review.
  5. Once Lithuania CASP authorized: migrate customers, wind down Estonian VASP (notify RAB).
  6. Total migration time: 5–9 months. Total cost: $40K–$80K incremental on top of existing Estonian setup.

FAQ — Lithuania vs Estonia crypto license

Why did crypto operators migrate from Estonia to Lithuania?

Estonia tightened its VASP regime substantially in 2022–2024 after high-profile failures: higher own funds (€125K–€350K vs Lithuania's standard MiCA €125K), mandatory local Estonian director, physical office requirement, RAB approval of all directors and UBOs. Combined with Lithuania's faster Bank of Lithuania reviews and deeper EMI banking ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome), Lithuania became the default Baltic CASP jurisdiction for new applicants in 2025–2026.

Lithuania CASP vs Estonia VASP — which is cheaper?

Lithuania is typically cheaper in total economic cost. Lithuania CASP setup: $50K–$120K + €125K paid-up capital. Estonia VASP setup: $30K–$70K headline + €100K paid-up + €125K–€350K own funds (held). Estonia's lower setup is offset by higher capital requirements and mandatory Estonian substance (local director, office) running $20K–$80K/year ongoing.

Do both Lithuania and Estonia grant EU MiCA passport?

Yes. Both Lithuania CASP and Estonia CASP (post-MiCA transition) grant the same EU passport across 27 EU member states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway. The MiCA framework is a harmonized EU regulation; the passport is identical regardless of which EU competent authority granted the CASP. Differences are entirely operational (cost, timeline, substance, banking).

Is Estonian VASP still being issued in 2026?

Limited. New Estonian VASPs are still issued, but RAB applies stricter substance scrutiny since 2022. From 30 December 2024, MiCA CASP authorization is the standard pathway for new applicants. Existing VASPs have until 30 June 2026 to transition to CASP under grandfathering. Many existing Estonian VASPs are choosing to migrate to Lithuania CASP instead of transitioning in Estonia.

What's Estonia's tax advantage vs Lithuania?

Estonia uniquely taxes corporate profits only when distributed (as dividends), not when earned. Retained earnings face 0% Estonian corporate tax. Distributions are taxed at 22%. Lithuania charges 15% (or 5% small entity) on profits as earned. For crypto businesses reinvesting profits, Estonia's structure compounds capital without annual tax drag. For operators distributing profits regularly, Lithuania's 15% (or 5%) often beats Estonia's 22% on distribution.

Which has stronger banking — Lithuania or Estonia?

Lithuania. The Lithuanian EMI ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome, plus traditional Citadele and SEB) is the deepest crypto-friendly banking environment in the EU. Estonian banks have tightened crypto onboarding significantly since 2023 — fewer Estonian banks now open accounts for new CASP entities, and onboarding takes 4–8 weeks vs Lithuania's 1–2 weeks for crypto-friendly EMIs.
