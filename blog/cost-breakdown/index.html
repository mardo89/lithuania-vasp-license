The capital question first — €50K vs €125K vs €150K

Before getting into setup costs, let's address the elephant in the room: the MiCA capital requirement. Lithuania CASP requires €50,000 (Class 1), €125,000 (Class 2), or €150,000 (Class 3) paid-up capital deposited into your Lithuanian UAB before authorization is granted.

Critical clarifications:

  • Capital is NOT consumed. It remains your company's working capital — you can use it for operations, salaries, infrastructure. You just have to maintain the minimum throughout operations.
  • Capital is held in the Lithuanian UAB's bank account at the moment of CASP application. Auditor confirms.
  • Falling below the minimum triggers Bank of Lithuania supervision actions including possible suspension of authorization.
  • Most operators set capital slightly above minimum (e.g., €130K–€140K for Class 2) to provide buffer against operational losses.

If you can't lock €125K of capital, Class 2 is not a viable option. Consider Class 1 (€50K), Lithuania VASP transition only (existing entities), or a different jurisdiction (Canada MSB at $0 capital, Panama at $0 capital).

Hidden costs founders forget

  1. Capital opportunity cost (~$6,700/year on €125K at 5%). Locked capital can't earn risk-free returns elsewhere. Over 5 years, that's $33K in opportunity cost vs jurisdictions with $0 capital.
  2. Lithuanian substance ongoing ($15K–$80K/year). Lithuanian-resident MLRO, Lithuanian board member, real Lithuanian office. Adds significantly vs purely-foreign management.
  3. Bank of Lithuania annual supervision fee (€2K–€8K). Volume-based; small CASPs at lower end.
  4. Annual independent audit ($10K–$25K). Mandatory for Lithuanian CASPs. Required Lithuanian-recognized auditor (BDO, KPMG, EY, Deloitte, PwC, or specialized smaller firms).
  5. Stablecoin issuance overhead ($100K+). If you issue or facilitate stablecoins, MiCA Title III/IV impose substantial separate authorization, reserve requirements, white papers.
  6. Travel Rule + DAC8 reporting tooling ($5K–$20K/year). EU Travel Rule + DAC8 tax reporting (effective 2026). Notabene, Sumsub Travel Rule, etc.
  7. EU passport notifications per country ($2K–$5K each). Each EU member state requires separate passport notification. Cumulative cost adds up if passporting into 10+ countries.

FAQ — Lithuania CASP cost breakdown

What's the absolute minimum to set up a Lithuania CASP?

~$50,000 + €50K capital for Class 1 (advisory/portfolio). For an actual crypto exchange (Class 2), realistic minimum is ~$85K + €125K capital. Anyone quoting Class 2 setup under $60K is omitting Lithuanian substance, real legal counsel, or institutional AML — your application will fail Bank of Lithuania review.

Why is the €125K capital not "consumed"?

The capital is paid into your Lithuanian UAB's bank account before CASP authorization is granted. After authorization, the capital remains your working capital — you can use it for operations, salaries, infrastructure. The €125K is a regulatory minimum to be maintained, not a fee paid to Bank of Lithuania. If your operations consume the capital, you must replenish to maintain MiCA compliance.

Why is Lithuania CASP cheaper than Cyprus CASP?

Three reasons: (1) lower legal-counsel rates (Lithuanian crypto lawyers ~$300–$500/hour vs Cypriot ~$400–$700/hour); (2) lower Bank of Lithuania fees (€5K–€10K vs CySEC €10K–€20K); (3) less-mature market (Cyprus has more applicants pushing up demand for crypto-specialist services). Both grant the same EU MiCA passport.

What about hidden Lithuanian-substance costs?

Bank of Lithuania expects meaningful Lithuanian substance: Lithuanian-resident MLRO ($1,500–$5,000/month) + Lithuanian board member or operations head ($2,000–$8,000/month) + real Lithuanian office (not virtual, $500–$2,000/month). Total annual substance: $15K–$80K. Smaller operators use fractional Lithuanian MLRO; larger operations hire full-time Lithuanian staff.

Is Lithuania CASP worth $85K + €125K vs Panama at $30K + $0?

Yes, IF you target EU retail customers. The MiCA passport across 27 EU member states is unique to EU CASPs — Panama doesn't grant it. For EU-focused crypto exchanges, Lithuania CASP is the most cost-effective EU-regulated option (cheaper than Cyprus, Germany, France, Ireland). For non-EU operators, Panama or Canada MSB win on pure economics.
