The 3 MiCA CASP service classes we authorize

MiCA defines three CASP classes by service scope and capital requirement. Choose the class that matches your actual activity — Bank of Lithuania scrutinizes any mismatch.

📊

CASP Class 1 (€50,000 capital)

Lighter authorization for advisory and portfolio services. Suitable for crypto investment advisors, robo-advisors, and discretionary portfolio managers — not for exchanges or custodians.

  • Reception & transmission of orders
  • Advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets
  • 4–6 month authorization timeline
From $40,000 setup + €50K capital

CASP Class 2 (€125,000 capital)

The most common class for crypto exchanges, brokers, and custodians. Covers Class 1 services plus custody, exchange, and order execution. Most international crypto operators target Class 2.

  • Custody & administration of crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets
From $50,000 setup + €125K capital
🏛️

CASP Class 3 (€150,000 capital)

Highest authorization tier — required for operating a multilateral trading platform (MTF/order book exchange). All Class 2 services plus running the trading platform itself.

  • Operation of trading platform for crypto-assets
  • All Class 1 + Class 2 services
  • 5–8 month authorization timeline
  • Heaviest market-abuse and surveillance obligations
From $80,000 setup + €150K capital

Glossary: key terms for Lithuania CASP authorization

MiCA introduced new vocabulary that's now standard across EU crypto regulation. These are the terms you'll encounter most often.

MiCA
Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation — EU Regulation 2023/1114 establishing the EU framework for crypto-asset issuers and service providers. Enforcement began 30 December 2024.
CASP
Crypto-Asset Service Provider — the MiCA term for a regulated crypto company. Replaces the pre-MiCA "VASP" terminology in the EU. Three classes by service scope and capital.
VASP
Virtual Asset Service Provider — FATF-aligned terminology used in pre-MiCA Lithuanian regulation. Now superseded by CASP for new applications; existing VASPs are in transition until 30 June 2026.
Bank of Lithuania
Lietuvos Bankas — the Lithuanian central bank and competent authority for CASP authorization under MiCA. Handles all crypto applications, supervision, and enforcement in Lithuania.
UAB
Uždaroji Akcinė Bendrovė — Lithuanian private limited company. The standard corporate vehicle for Lithuania CASP. Requires €2,500 minimum share capital for incorporation.
EU Passport
Right to provide crypto services across all 27 EU member states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway (EEA) from a single authorization. Activated via notification to Bank of Lithuania, which informs receiving competent authorities.
Grandfathering
18-month transitional period (30 December 2024 – 30 June 2026) during which pre-MiCA Lithuanian VASPs can continue operating while obtaining CASP authorization. After 30 June 2026, all Lithuanian crypto businesses must hold CASP authorization.
5AMLD / 6AMLD
5th and 6th EU Anti-Money Laundering Directives — the EU AML framework that applies to all CASPs. Requires KYC, transaction monitoring, suspicious-activity reporting to FCIS (Financial Crime Investigation Service of Lithuania).

Lithuania VASP/CASP — frequently asked questions

The most common questions from founders considering Lithuania for their EU-regulated crypto company in 2026.

How much does a Lithuania VASP/CASP license cost in 2026?

Lithuania CASP (MiCA) authorization costs $50,000–$120,000 in year one all-in: €5,000–€10,000 Bank of Lithuania license fee, €15,000–€30,000 AML/KYC program development, €10,000–€25,000 legal counsel for application, €5,000–€10,000 Lithuanian UAB formation, plus €125,000 minimum paid-up capital that must be deposited (not consumed). Annual maintenance runs $20,000–$50,000. Full cost breakdown →

What is the minimum capital for Lithuania CASP under MiCA?

MiCA sets three CASP classes with different capital requirements: Class 1 — €50,000 (advice, portfolio management); Class 2 — €125,000 (Class 1 + custody, exchange, execution); Class 3 — €150,000 (Class 2 + operation of trading platform). Most international crypto exchanges target Class 2 or Class 3. Capital must be paid up before authorization is granted and maintained throughout operations.

How long does Lithuania CASP authorization take?

Lithuania CASP authorization takes 4–8 months end-to-end: 1–2 months for application preparation (UAB formation, AML program, business plan, financial projections), then 20–25 weeks for Bank of Lithuania review under MiCA Article 62. Existing Lithuanian VASPs have until 30 June 2026 to transition to CASP under the grandfathering provisions.

Does Lithuania VASP/CASP provide an EU passport?

Yes. Once authorized as a CASP under MiCA, a Lithuanian crypto company can passport its services across all 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway (EEA). The passport is granted via notification to the Bank of Lithuania, which informs receiving competent authorities. This is the primary value proposition vs Panama or Canada — direct EU market access from a single authorization.

When does MiCA grandfathering for Lithuanian VASPs end?

30 June 2026. MiCA enforcement began on 30 December 2024 for CASPs. Lithuanian VASPs registered under the pre-MiCA framework have an 18-month grandfathering period to obtain CASP authorization. After 30 June 2026, all Lithuanian crypto businesses must hold CASP authorization or cease operations. Many Lithuanian VASPs are currently in the transition process.

Lithuania VASP vs Estonia VASP — which is better in 2026?

Lithuania has become the dominant Baltic VASP jurisdiction in 2026. Estonia tightened its VASP regime significantly in 2022–2024, requiring expensive in-country substance and triggering many operators to migrate to Lithuania. Estonia still has VASPs, but Lithuania is preferred for new authorizations because of: (1) clearer Bank of Lithuania guidance, (2) more flexible MiCA transition pathway, (3) deeper crypto-friendly banking ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay).

Is the Lithuania VASP framework still available in 2026?

For new authorizations, no — since 30 December 2024, only CASP authorization under MiCA is available for new applicants. The pre-MiCA Lithuania VASP regime is closed to new entrants. Existing Lithuanian VASPs registered before 30 December 2024 can continue under the grandfathering provisions until 30 June 2026.

What corporate vehicle is required for Lithuania CASP?

A Lithuanian UAB (Uždaroji Akcinė Bendrovė — private limited company) is the standard corporate form. UAB requires €2,500 minimum share capital for incorporation, but CASP authorization separately requires €50,000–€150,000 paid-up capital depending on service class. UAB formation takes 1–2 weeks; CASP application is then submitted with the UAB as the applicant.
